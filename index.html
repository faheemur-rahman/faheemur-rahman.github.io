PhD Student, Electrical and Computer Engineering

I am a PhD student working on secure memristive computing hardware, with interests in analog in-memory computing, hardware security, memristive crossbar arrays, and circuit-level modeling.

My research focuses on building efficient and secure hardware systems for emerging computing platforms, including 3D-stacked memristive architectures, SPICE-based analysis, and attack-aware design strategies.

Before starting my PhD, I worked in the semiconductor industry on schematic design, layout, and simulation.

Research Interests

My research sits at the intersection of circuit design, hardware security, and emerging memory-based computing. I am particularly interested in how device- and circuit-level design choices affect security, performance, and scalability.

Secure Memristive Computing

Designing secure memristive crossbar systems for analog in-memory computing, including confidentiality-aware storage and attack-resilient organization.

Hardware Security

Studying threats such as probing, model extraction, and physical fault injection, and developing circuit-level protection mechanisms.

3D Crossbar Architectures

Exploring multi-tier memristive structures, TSV-based interconnects, and partitioned weight storage for improved security and design tradeoffs.

SPICE and Design Automation

Building HSPICE-based simulation workflows, netlist automation, parameter sweeps, and measurement frameworks for large-scale hardware analysis.
