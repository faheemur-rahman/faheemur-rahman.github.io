I am a PhD student at UMass Amherst working at the intersection of circuit design, hardware security, and emerging memory-based computing. My research focuses on secure memristive crossbar architectures, analog in-memory computing, and circuit-level modeling for efficient and trustworthy hardware systems.

My work includes 2D and 3D memristive crossbar arrays, HSPICE-based evaluation, design automation, and security mechanisms such as obfuscation, watermarking, and fault-aware monitoring.

Research Interests

My research sits at the intersection of VLSI, hardware security, and emerging computing hardware. I am particularly interested in how device- and circuit-level design choices affect security, performance, and scalability in memristive and AI-oriented systems.

Secure Memristive Computing

Designing secure memristive crossbar systems for analog in-memory computing, including confidentiality-aware storage, attack-resilient organization, and protected weight mapping.

Hardware Security

Studying threats such as probing, model extraction, and physical fault injection, and developing circuit-level protection, sensing, and monitoring mechanisms.

3D Crossbar Architectures

Exploring multi-tier memristive structures, TSV-based integration, and partitioned weight storage for improved confidentiality and system-level design tradeoffs.

SPICE and Design Automation

Building HSPICE-based simulation workflows, Python automation, and measurement frameworks for large-scale circuit evaluation and security-focused experimentation.
